# Tajweed Scholars Locked Phase 1 Rules

These rules are approved and locked. Do not override them with general strategic recommendations.

## Business model

- Tajweed Scholars provides live, private, one-to-one online Quran classes.
- Every regular class is 30 minutes and currently takes place through Zoom.
- Primary audiences are children and adult learners. The website must also suit teenagers and parents and must not feel children-only.

## Trial model

- The official offer is three free trial classes. Do not reduce it to an assessment or fewer trials.
- Trial 1 includes official placement assessment and a real mini-lesson. There is no separate assessment class.
- Trial 2 is a full learning experience.
- Trial 3 includes a final lesson, progress recap, and enrollment recommendation.
- The primary CTA is **“Book 3 Free Trial Classes.”** Do not replace it with “Book a Free Assessment.”

## Credentials

- Tajweed Scholars is led by a founder with a complete verified Sanad.
- Teachers must hold verified Sanad/Ijazah credentials before assignment.
- Never invent names, profiles, certificate numbers, universities, affiliations, qualifications, statistics, testimonials, reviews, student numbers, teacher photographs, or simulated identities.

## Programs

- Kids Quran Classes serves ages 4–15.
- Adult Quran Classes is a separate private adult pathway.
- Tajweed Course and Hifz Program are active core programs.
- Arabic Language is public but Phase 1 enrollment is consultation-based.
- Islamic Studies is public as an add-on with eligible Quran programs.
- Arabic Language and Islamic Studies must not have equal homepage prominence with the four core programs.

## Pricing

- Public plans start from $40 per month.
- Plans support one to five days per week plus Weekend 1-Day and Weekend 2-Day options.
- Do not show hourly or per-class pricing.
- Do not expose internal prices through a public toggle.
- Do not invent discounts or payment processors.

## Phase 1 exclusions

- No parent dashboard, student portal, mobile app, AI progress reports, online checkout, fake automation, login system, Gemini/AI feature, or fake contact details.
- Do not publish policy pages until founder approval.
- Do not claim legal review.

## Progress wording

Use: regular progress updates, attendance notes, homework updates, teacher feedback, and clear next goals.

Do not claim automated dashboards, gamification, reports after every class, or non-operational technology.

## Design direction

The direction is **quiet scholarly confidence**: deep forest green, warm ivory/parchment, charcoal, restrained muted brass, editorial typography, readable headings, generous whitespace, subtle Islamic geometry, strong hierarchy, minimal cards, calm transitions, and clearly labeled mature photography placeholders when needed.

Avoid childish icons, cartoons, generic ed-tech styling, SaaS dashboards, simulated classroom interfaces, excessive badges/gradients/card grids, tiny uppercase labels, monospace interface styling, and long visible copy blocks.

## Engineering rules

- Keep React, Vite, TypeScript, Tailwind, and the current router during this redesign.
- Do not edit Free Trial, Pricing, Contact, program, or policy pages unless a later user request explicitly changes the scope.
- Do not commit or push changes.
- Run TypeScript validation and a production build after editing.
- Report every changed file.
